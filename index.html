Bessie Coleman

The first black woman to have a pilot's license

1892 - 1926

"The air is the only place free from prejudices. I knew we had no aviators, neither men nor women, and I knew the Race needed to be represented along this most important line, so I thought it my duty to risk my life to learn aviation and to encourage flying among men and women of our Race who are so far behind the White race in this modern study."
- Bessie Coleman

1892
Born in Atlanta, Texas, United States on 26th January, 1982. Her parents were Susan and George Coleman.
1894
Her family moved to Waxahachie, Texas where they lived as sharecroppers.
1898
She attended a segregated, one room school where she walked 4 miles everyday to get there, she loved to read and was a great math student.
1901
Her father who was part Native-American returned to Indian Territory (now know as Oklahoma) alone to avoid segregation in order to find better opportunities, his wife did not want to go so stayed behind with their children.
1910
With the savings she had, Bessie enrolled in the Oklahoma Colored Agricultural and Normal University (now Langston University) but only completed one term before her savings depleted.
1915
She moved to Chicago, Illinois and lived with her brothers, she became a manicurist, this is where she heard stories of pilots during the war, she decided to become a pilot so took a second job at a chilli shop in order to save enough money to become a pilot.
1920
Bessie was unable to study in America due to being a black woman, she learnt French and on 20th November, 1920 she travelled to Paris so she could earn her pilot's license.
1921
On 15th June, 1921, Bessie became the first Black woman to earn a pilot's license and international aviation license from the Frédération Aéronautique Internationale. She sailed to America on 21st September, 1921 and became a media sensation on arrival.
1922
In February 1922, she returned to Europe to take more advanced flying lessons in order to perform stunt tricks. She made her first appearance on 3rd September 1922 in an American airshow at an event honouring veterans of the all-black 369th Infantry Regiment of World War I.
1923
She suffered her first accident in February 1923 after her airplane engine suddenly stopped working, she was lucky as she was able to fully recover as she only suffered a few broken bones. After she recovered, she continued flying.
1926
On 30th April 1926, she was testing her new plane in preparation for an air show on the 1st of May, she was in the passenger seat but wasn't wearing a seatbelt as she was looking over the side in order to find a parachute jump spot. Unfortunately there was a wrench in the engine which caused the engine to fail and the plane to flip, she fell out and fell to her death. She was 34
1995
The "Bessie Coleman Stamp" was issued by the U.S Postal Service in her honour.
2006
She was inducted into the National Aviation Hall of Fame

Although Bessie Coleman's career was cut short, she inspired many people to reach for their dreams no matter what may be in their way. She has received numerous honours in recognition of her work and contribution to society.
